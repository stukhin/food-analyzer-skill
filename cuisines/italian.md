# Italian

## Profile summary

Italian splits cleanly on the sauce axis. **Light side:** olive oil + tomato + seafood + vegetable + light wine sauces (aglio e olio, pomodoro, marinara, vongole, scoglio, puttanesca). **Heavy side:** cream + cheese as the structural base (carbonara, cacio e pepe, alfredo, 4-formaggi, lasagne with béchamel, truffle-cream). The keyword test: if "cream" or "cheese-as-base" appears → heavy; if "olive oil + garlic + tomato/wine" → light.

Critical for modifications: **cream/cheese pastas have the sauce cooked into the dish — the kitchen cannot serve them "without cheese" or "sauce on side"**. The only meaningful modifications are portion size or ordering a different dish.

## Standard dishes

### Pasta aglio e olio
- **Components:** spaghetti, olive oil, garlic, chili flakes, parsley
- **Typical cooking:** boiled pasta + sautéed garlic in olive oil
- **Typical macros (standard 100g dry / ~250g cooked):**
  - Calories: 480–580 kcal
  - Fat: 18–24g (sat 3–4g) — almost all from olive oil
  - Carbs: 65–75g (sugar <2g)
  - Protein: 12–15g
  - Fiber: 3–4g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less oil" — always
  - "no chili" — always
- **Heuristics:** quality-aware fat (olive oil neutral/positive vs animal fat)
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Pasta pomodoro / marinara
- **Components:** pasta, tomato sauce (tomato + onion + garlic + basil + olive oil)
- **Typical cooking:** boiled pasta + light tomato sauce
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 380–480 kcal
  - Fat: 8–14g (sat 1–2g)
  - Carbs: 65–75g (sugar 8–12g from tomato)
  - Protein: 11–14g
  - Fiber: 4–6g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "no parmesan on top" — always
  - "less oil" — sometimes
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Pasta vongole / scoglio / seafood
- **Components:** pasta, clams or mixed seafood, olive oil, garlic, white wine, parsley
- **Typical cooking:** boiled pasta + sautéed seafood in oil + wine
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 450–560 kcal
  - Fat: 12–18g (sat 2–3g)
  - Carbs: 60–70g (sugar <3g)
  - Protein: 22–30g (high — seafood)
  - Fiber: 2–3g
  - Sodium: 600–900mg (seafood + sauce)
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less oil" — sometimes
  - "no salt added" — sometimes (but seafood naturally has sodium)
- **Heuristics:** lean protein bonus; sodium watch for hypertension
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Pasta puttanesca
- **Components:** pasta, tomato, olives, capers, anchovies, garlic, olive oil, chili
- **Typical cooking:** boiled pasta + cooked tomato-olive sauce
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 420–520 kcal
  - Fat: 14–20g (sat 2–3g)
  - Carbs: 60–70g (sugar 6–10g)
  - Protein: 13–18g
  - Fiber: 4–5g
  - **Sodium: 1100–1500mg** (anchovies + olives + capers — very high)
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "no anchovy" — always (but changes the dish)
  - "no capers" — sometimes
- **Heuristics:** sodium red flag — call out for hypertension profiles
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Carbonara
- **Components:** pasta, guanciale or pancetta, egg yolks, pecorino romano, black pepper
- **Typical cooking:** boiled pasta tossed with rendered guanciale + raw egg yolks (cooked by pasta heat) + grated cheese
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 580–740 kcal
  - Fat: 28–42g (sat 12–18g) — heavy: cured pork + egg yolk + hard cheese
  - Carbs: 55–70g (sugar 2–4g)
  - Protein: 22–28g
  - Fiber: 2–3g
  - Sodium: 800–1100mg (cheese + cured meat)
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "no cheese" — **NEVER** (cheese IS the dish)
  - "no bacon/guanciale" — **NEVER** (defines carbonara)
  - "sauce on side" — **NEVER** (egg + cheese cooked into pasta)
  - "less cheese on top" — sometimes (the extra grated topping can be cut)
  - "half portion" — always
- **Heuristics:** hidden-fat (cured pork + cheese melt); no Rule-9 violations allowed
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Cacio e pepe
- **Components:** pasta, pecorino romano, black pepper, pasta water
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 550–680 kcal
  - Fat: 22–32g (sat 13–18g) — almost all from cheese
  - Carbs: 65–75g (sugar <3g)
  - Protein: 22–28g
  - Sodium: 900–1300mg
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "no cheese" — **NEVER** (cheese IS the dish, literally translates "cheese and pepper")
  - "less cheese" — sometimes (but changes character)
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Alfredo / fettuccine alfredo
- **Components:** pasta, butter, heavy cream, parmesan
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 650–820 kcal
  - Fat: 38–52g (sat 22–30g) — among the heaviest pasta dishes
  - Carbs: 55–70g
  - Protein: 18–24g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less cream" / "no cream" — **NEVER** (cream IS the sauce)
  - "half portion" — always
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Lasagne bolognese with béchamel
- **Components:** pasta sheets, ragù bolognese (meat + tomato), béchamel (butter + flour + milk), mozzarella, parmesan
- **Macros (one slice 250g):**
  - Calories: 480–620 kcal
  - Fat: 22–32g (sat 12–18g)
  - Carbs: 40–55g
  - Protein: 22–30g
  - Sodium: 700–1000mg
- **Modifications possible:**
  - Structural — sauce cooked into the dish, **cannot modify cream/cheese**
  - "smaller slice" — sometimes
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Pizza margherita
- **Components:** thin crust, tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella, basil, olive oil
- **Macros (one personal pizza, ~250g):**
  - Calories: 580–740 kcal
  - Fat: 18–28g (sat 9–14g) — mostly mozzarella
  - Carbs: 75–90g
  - Protein: 24–32g
  - Sodium: 1100–1500mg (cheese + sauce)
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "light cheese / extra-light cheese" — sometimes
  - "no extra oil" — always
  - "half portion / share" — always
- **Heuristics:** salt watch; sodium can drag verdict
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Pizza quattro formaggi
- **Components:** crust, four cheeses (often mozzarella + gorgonzola + parmesan + fontina)
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 720–920 kcal
  - Fat: 32–46g (sat 18–26g) — heaviest pizza class
  - Carbs: 70–85g
  - Protein: 30–40g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less cheese" — sometimes (but changes the dish)
  - "share" — always
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Risotto (depends on style)
- **Components:** arborio rice, broth, butter, parmesan (always); plus mushroom / seafood / vegetables / saffron
- **Typical cooking:** slow-stirred starch release with butter at finish ("mantecatura")
- **Macros (mushroom risotto, ~280g):**
  - Calories: 480–620 kcal
  - Fat: 16–26g (sat 9–14g) — butter + cheese finish
  - Carbs: 65–80g
  - Protein: 12–18g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less mantecatura" / "less butter at finish" — sometimes (changes texture)
  - "no parmesan on top" — sometimes
- **Heuristics:** hidden fat in mantecatura
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM (huge variability)

### Caprese salad
- **Components:** tomato, fresh mozzarella, basil, olive oil
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 280–380 kcal
  - Fat: 18–26g (sat 8–13g) — mozzarella + olive oil
  - Carbs: 8–12g (sugar 5–8g from tomato)
  - Protein: 14–20g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less mozzarella" — always
  - "less oil" — always
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Minestrone
- **Components:** vegetable broth, mixed vegetables, beans, small pasta, optional parmesan
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 180–260 kcal
  - Fat: 4–8g (sat 1–2g)
  - Carbs: 28–38g
  - Protein: 8–12g
  - Fiber: 8–12g (excellent)
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "no parmesan" — always
  - "no oil drizzle" — always
- **Heuristics:** fiber bonus; lean protein from beans
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Bruschetta (tomato + olive oil)
- **Components:** grilled bread, raw tomato, garlic, basil, olive oil
- **Macros (2 pieces):**
  - Calories: 220–320 kcal
  - Fat: 10–16g (sat 1–2g)
  - Carbs: 28–38g
  - Protein: 5–8g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less oil" — always
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Tiramisu
- **Components:** ladyfingers + coffee + mascarpone + egg yolk + sugar + cocoa
- **Macros (1 slice ~120g):**
  - Calories: 360–460 kcal
  - Fat: 22–32g (sat 14–20g)
  - Carbs: 30–40g (sugar 22–30g)
  - Protein: 6–10g
- **Modifications possible:** none (structural)
- **Heuristics:** indulgence framework candidate
- **Confidence:** HIGH

## Common red flags in this cuisine

- **Cream-based pastas** (carbonara, cacio e pepe, alfredo, 4-formaggi, tartufata) — sauce IS the dish, not modifiable, very heavy in saturated fat
- **Cheese on top of everything** — restaurants often add grated parmesan/pecorino post-plating; ask to skip if not central to the dish
- **Stuffed pasta in cream sauce** (ravioli/tortelli/agnolotti with cream/butter/sage) — double load: filling + cream sauce
- **Cured meats** (prosciutto, salami, mortadella, speck) — high sodium + saturated fat
- **Tiramisu / panna cotta / cannoli** — concentrated cream + sugar
- **Olive oil drizzle at restaurant volumes** — quality fat but 1–3 tbsp can add 15–40g fat invisibly

---

## Common safe choices

- **Pasta with olive oil + tomato + seafood / vegetable** sauces (aglio e olio, vongole, pomodoro, primavera)
- **Minestrone** — fiber-rich, low fat, well-tolerated
- **Grilled fish with vegetables** (orata, branzino al forno + vegetable contorno)
- **Insalata mista / caprese with less mozzarella** — quick light start
- **Bruschetta pomodoro** — small portion appetizer
- **Bistecca with side of vegetables (no fries)** — lean cut, grilled, sauce on side
