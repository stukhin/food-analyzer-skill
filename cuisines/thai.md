# Thai

## Profile summary

Thai cuisine has two big forks. **Broth fork:** tom yum (clear, sour, spicy — light) vs tom kha (coconut-cream-based — heavy). **Curry fork:** stir-fries (light–moderate) vs coconut curries (green / red / massaman / Panang — heavy, coconut milk + cream). Dessert side is dominated by coconut + palm sugar.

Other recurring traps: sweet sauces (palm sugar + tamarind in pad thai, sweet chili everywhere), deep-fried items (soft-shell crab, spring rolls when fried), peanut as a stealth fat source.

## Standard dishes

### Tom yum (clear, no coconut)
- **Components:** clear broth + lemongrass + galangal + lime leaves + chili + lime juice + fish sauce + shrimp / chicken / mushroom
- **Macros (one bowl ~300ml):**
  - Calories: 90–160 kcal
  - Fat: 2–5g
  - Carbs: 6–12g
  - Protein: 12–20g
  - Sodium: 1200–1800mg (fish sauce + broth)
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less chili" — always
  - "less fish sauce" — sometimes (changes flavor)
- **Heuristics:** broth fork (light end); sodium watch
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Tom kha (coconut)
- **Components:** coconut milk + galangal + lemongrass + chili + chicken or shrimp
- **Macros (one bowl ~300ml):**
  - Calories: 320–460 kcal
  - Fat: 22–32g (sat 18–26g) — almost all from coconut milk
  - Carbs: 10–16g
  - Protein: 14–22g
  - Sodium: 1200–1800mg
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less coconut" — sometimes (changes character)
- **Heuristics:** broth fork (heavy end); coconut = saturated despite being plant
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Som tam (green papaya salad)
- **Components:** shredded green papaya + tomato + long beans + dried shrimp / peanuts + chili + lime + palm sugar + fish sauce
- **Macros (one plate ~200g):**
  - Calories: 120–200 kcal
  - Fat: 3–7g (sat 1–2g; some from peanuts)
  - Carbs: 18–28g (sugar 8–14g from palm sugar)
  - Protein: 6–12g
  - Fiber: 5–8g
  - Sodium: 900–1400mg
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less palm sugar" — always
  - "no peanuts" — always (allergy or fat-watching)
  - "less chili" — always
- **Heuristics:** sugar load from palm sugar; sodium from fish sauce
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Pad thai
- **Components:** rice noodles stir-fried with eggs, shrimp or chicken, tofu, bean sprouts, in tamarind + palm sugar + fish sauce + chili sauce, topped with peanuts
- **Macros (one plate ~350g):**
  - Calories: 580–760 kcal
  - Fat: 22–32g (sat 5–8g)
  - Carbs: 70–90g (sugar 14–22g from palm sugar + tamarind)
  - Protein: 22–32g
  - Sodium: 1400–2000mg
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less sauce" — sometimes
  - "less peanuts on top" — always
  - "no sugar added" — sometimes
- **Heuristics:** sweet sauce sugar load; hidden oil from stir-fry
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Pad see ew
- **Components:** wide rice noodles stir-fried with Chinese broccoli, egg, soy sauce + dark sweet soy + protein
- **Macros (one plate ~350g):**
  - Calories: 520–680 kcal
  - Fat: 18–28g (sat 4–7g)
  - Carbs: 70–90g (sugar 10–16g from sweet soy)
  - Protein: 22–32g
  - Sodium: 1600–2400mg
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less sweet soy" — sometimes
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Green curry
- **Components:** coconut milk + green curry paste + chicken / fish / vegetables + Thai eggplant + basil
- **Macros (one bowl ~350g, with rice ~200g):**
  - Calories: 580–780 kcal
  - Fat: 28–42g (sat 18–28g) — coconut milk dominant
  - Carbs: 55–75g
  - Protein: 22–32g
  - Sodium: 1400–2000mg
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less coconut" / "thinner curry" — sometimes
  - "less rice" — always
- **Heuristics:** coconut = saturated fat heavy
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Red curry / Panang curry
- Similar to green; Panang adds peanut paste → slightly higher fat
- **Macros:** Calories 600–800 kcal, Fat 30–44g (sat 19–28g)
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Massaman curry
- **Components:** coconut milk + massaman paste (with peanuts, cinnamon, cardamom) + slow-cooked beef + potato
- **Macros (one bowl ~400g, with rice):**
  - Calories: 680–880 kcal
  - Fat: 32–46g (sat 20–28g)
  - Carbs: 60–80g (sugar 10–14g)
  - Protein: 28–38g
- **Heuristics:** richest curry category
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Larb (minced meat salad)
- **Components:** minced chicken / pork / beef + toasted rice powder + lime + chili + mint + shallot + fish sauce
- **Macros (one plate ~200g):**
  - Calories: 220–340 kcal
  - Fat: 12–20g (sat 4–7g) — depends on meat cut
  - Carbs: 8–14g
  - Protein: 24–34g
  - Sodium: 1100–1700mg
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "leaner meat" — sometimes
- **Heuristics:** lean meat option = lighter
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Grilled fish (pla pao) — whole fish grilled in salt crust
- **Components:** whole fish (often sea bass, snakehead) grilled in salt crust, served with chili-lime dip
- **Macros (one fish ~250g):**
  - Calories: 300–420 kcal
  - Fat: 12–20g (sat 3–5g; often beneficial omega-3)
  - Carbs: 0g
  - Protein: 40–55g
- **Heuristics:** lean / fatty fish depending on species; cooking ladder (grilled)
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Spring rolls (fresh) vs spring rolls (fried)
- **Fresh (gỏi cuốn / Thai version with rice paper):**
  - Calories: 80–140 kcal/roll, Fat: 1–3g, Carbs: 14–22g
  - Heuristics: light, fiber
- **Fried:**
  - Calories: 150–220 kcal/roll, Fat: 8–14g (sat 2–3g), Carbs: 12–20g
  - Heuristics: deep-fry penalty
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Soft-shell crab dishes (deep-fried)
- **Components:** soft-shell crab battered + deep-fried, often served on salad or with sauce
- **Macros (one plate with crab):**
  - Calories: 480–680 kcal
  - Fat: 30–45g (sat 5–8g) — oil absorbed in batter
  - Carbs: 25–40g
  - Protein: 20–30g
- **Heuristics:** cooking ladder (deep-fry); often deceptively in "salad" category
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Mango sticky rice
- **Components:** sticky rice steamed in coconut milk + ripe mango + extra coconut cream + toasted sesame
- **Macros (one serving ~250g):**
  - Calories: 420–560 kcal
  - Fat: 16–24g (sat 13–20g) — coconut + coconut cream finish
  - Carbs: 65–85g (sugar 32–45g from mango + added sugar)
  - Protein: 4–7g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less coconut cream on top" — sometimes
  - "half portion" — always
- **Heuristics:** indulgence framework candidate; saturated fat + sugar combo
- **Confidence:** HIGH

## Common red flags in this cuisine

- **Coconut milk / coconut cream** in soups, curries, desserts — saturated fat heavy despite plant source
- **Palm sugar in everything** — som tam, pad thai, sweet soy dishes, desserts
- **Sweet-and-sour sauces** — high sugar
- **Deep-fried items** (spring rolls fried, soft-shell crab, fried banana)
- **Peanut sauces** — flavor anchor but high in fat
- **Fish sauce sodium** — invisible 700–1200mg per dish
- **"Curry rice" combo** — heavy curry + 200g rice = 800+ calories easily

---

## Common safe choices

- **Tom yum (clear) with shrimp / chicken / mushroom** — light, warming, low calorie
- **Som tam with reduced sugar + no peanuts** — fiber, low calorie
- **Grilled fish (pla pao) + rice** — lean protein
- **Larb (lean chicken / fish)** — minced lean protein salad
- **Fresh spring rolls** — light wrap option
- **Stir-fried vegetables with garlic (pad pak)** — vegetable side
