# Japanese

## Profile summary

Japanese cuisine is bimodal. **Light side:** raw lean fish (sashimi, nigiri), clear-broth soups (miso, dashi-based), steamed dishes (chawanmushi), grilled fish with miso marinade. **Heavy side:** deep-fried (tempura, tonkatsu, karaage, agedashi), pork-bone ramen (tonkotsu), pork-belly chashu, fatty tuna (otoro), curry roux.

The **ramen fork** is critical: clear shoyu/shio is the lighter end; tonkotsu pork-bone is among the heaviest soups in any cuisine. The **brothless noodle trap**: mazesoba / abura soba contain 2–3 tbsp aroma oil pooled at the bottom — instruct "don't drink/scrape the bottom".

## Standard dishes

### Sashimi (lean fish — tuna akami, salmon, hamachi, sea bream, scallop)
- **Components:** raw fish, soy sauce (separate), wasabi, optional ginger
- **Typical cooking:** raw, sliced
- **Macros (5–7 pieces, ~80g):**
  - Calories: 90–140 kcal
  - Fat: 1–4g (sat <1g; salmon higher with omega-3)
  - Carbs: 0g
  - Protein: 18–22g
  - Sodium: from soy sauce ~600–900mg per tbsp
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "low-sodium soy" / "use less soy" — always
  - "ponzu instead of soy" — sometimes
- **Heuristics:** lean protein; salmon omega-3 bonus
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Nigiri (lean fish on rice)
- **Components:** vinegared rice + raw fish slice
- **Macros (6 pieces nigiri ~150g):**
  - Calories: 250–340 kcal
  - Fat: 4–8g (sat 1–2g)
  - Carbs: 38–48g (sugar 3–5g from sushi rice vinegar)
  - Protein: 16–22g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less rice" / "sashimi instead" — always
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Fatty tuna (otoro / chutoro) nigiri or sashimi
- **Components:** raw belly tuna (high marbling)
- **Macros (3 pieces nigiri ~75g):**
  - Calories: 280–360 kcal
  - Fat: 16–24g (sat 4–6g; rest unsaturated incl. omega-3)
  - Protein: 14–18g
- **Heuristics:** quality fat adjustment — high-fat fish but omega-3 dominant
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Miso soup (clear)
- **Components:** dashi (kombu + bonito), miso paste, tofu, wakame, scallions
- **Macros (1 bowl ~200ml):**
  - Calories: 40–70 kcal
  - Fat: 1–3g
  - Carbs: 4–7g
  - Protein: 3–5g
  - Sodium: 700–1000mg (miso)
- **Modifications possible:**
  - none structural
- **Heuristics:** sodium watch
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Chawanmushi (savory steamed egg custard)
- **Components:** dashi + egg + tiny shrimp / chicken / mushroom, steamed
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 90–130 kcal
  - Fat: 4–7g (sat 1–2g)
  - Carbs: 2–4g
  - Protein: 9–13g
- **Modifications possible:** none
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Shio / shoyu ramen (clear broth)
- **Components:** chicken or seafood broth + wheat noodles + chashu (1–2 slices) + soft-boiled egg + scallion + nori
- **Macros (one bowl ~600ml):**
  - Calories: 480–620 kcal
  - Fat: 14–22g (sat 4–7g)
  - Carbs: 60–75g
  - Protein: 22–30g
  - Sodium: 1800–2800mg (broth salt — high)
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less broth oil" — sometimes (kitchens can scoop less surface oil)
  - "no chashu" — sometimes
  - "don't drink the broth" — at-the-table lever, cuts ~30% sodium
- **Heuristics:** sodium dominant; broth-discrimination matters (clear vs not)
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Tonkotsu ramen (pork-bone broth)
- **Components:** emulsified pork-bone broth + noodles + chashu + egg + garnishes
- **Macros (one bowl ~600ml):**
  - Calories: 680–900 kcal
  - Fat: 28–42g (sat 12–18g) — emulsified pork bone fat
  - Carbs: 65–80g
  - Protein: 28–38g
  - Sodium: 2200–3200mg
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "don't drink the broth" — most effective lever, cuts ~40% fat + sodium
  - "no chashu" — sometimes
- **Heuristics:** broth fork (heavy end); hidden fat in emulsion
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Mazesoba / abura soba (brothless oil noodles)
- **Components:** noodles tossed with aroma oil (often 2–3 tbsp) + soy + minced pork + egg yolk + garnishes
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 620–820 kcal
  - Fat: 26–40g (sat 7–11g) — most from aroma oil at bottom
  - Carbs: 70–85g
  - Protein: 22–30g
  - Sodium: 1500–2200mg
- **Modifications possible:**
  - **"don't scrape the oily bottom"** — cuts ~10–15g fat
  - "less oil from start" — sometimes (rare in restaurants)
- **Heuristics:** hidden fat critical — this is the canonical "oil pool at bottom" trap
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Tempura (mixed)
- **Components:** seafood / vegetables battered (flour + cold water + egg) and deep-fried
- **Macros (5–6 pieces, ~150g):**
  - Calories: 380–520 kcal
  - Fat: 22–32g (sat 3–5g — oil absorbed)
  - Carbs: 30–45g
  - Protein: 12–18g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - none structural (deep-fried is the dish)
  - "smaller portion / share" — always
- **Heuristics:** cooking ladder (deep-fry penalty); quality fat (depends on oil)
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Tonkatsu (breaded deep-fried pork cutlet)
- **Components:** pork loin (or fillet) + panko breading + deep-fried + tonkatsu sauce
- **Macros (one cutlet 180g):**
  - Calories: 540–700 kcal
  - Fat: 32–46g (sat 10–14g)
  - Carbs: 25–35g (sugar 4–8g from sauce)
  - Protein: 30–40g
  - Sodium: 800–1200mg
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "sauce on side" — always (the brown sauce is post-served)
  - "skip the breading" — never (defines tonkatsu)
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Karaage (Japanese fried chicken)
- **Components:** chicken thigh marinated in soy + ginger + garlic, dusted in starch, deep-fried
- **Macros (5–6 pieces, ~150g):**
  - Calories: 380–500 kcal
  - Fat: 22–32g (sat 5–8g)
  - Carbs: 12–18g
  - Protein: 28–36g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "share" — always
- **Heuristics:** deep-fry penalty; thigh = fattier cut
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Yakitori (grilled chicken skewers)
- **Components:** chicken parts (thigh, breast, skin, liver, gizzard, hearts) grilled over coals, with tare (sweet soy glaze) or shio (salt)
- **Macros (3 skewers ~120g, mixed cuts):**
  - Calories: 220–320 kcal
  - Fat: 10–18g (sat 3–5g) — varies hugely by cut
  - Carbs: 4–10g (sugar 3–8g if tare-glazed)
  - Protein: 22–30g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "shio instead of tare" — always (cuts sugar)
  - "lean cuts only (breast, hearts)" — always
  - "no skin skewer" — always
- **Heuristics:** cut discrimination; cooking ladder (grilled = neutral)
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Saba / gindara saikyo-yaki (miso-marinated grilled fish)
- **Components:** mackerel or black cod marinated in saikyo miso, grilled
- **Macros (one fillet ~120g):**
  - Calories: 240–320 kcal
  - Fat: 14–22g (sat 3–5g; mostly omega-3)
  - Carbs: 4–8g (sugar 3–6g from miso marinade)
  - Protein: 22–28g
  - Sodium: 600–900mg
- **Modifications possible:** none (already light)
- **Heuristics:** omega-3 bonus; one of the best protein options in Japanese cuisine
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Chashu (braised pork belly slices)
- **Components:** pork belly slow-braised in soy + mirin + sake + ginger
- **Macros (3 slices ~80g):**
  - Calories: 240–320 kcal
  - Fat: 18–26g (sat 7–10g)
  - Protein: 12–18g
  - Sodium: 500–800mg
- **Heuristics:** cut discrimination (belly = fattiest); often add-on, removable from ramen
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Gyudon (beef bowl)
- **Components:** thin-sliced beef (often short plate / yakiniku cut, fattier) + onion + sweet soy sauce + rice + optional raw egg
- **Macros (one bowl ~400g):**
  - Calories: 580–740 kcal
  - Fat: 18–28g (sat 8–12g)
  - Carbs: 75–90g (sugar 12–18g from sauce)
  - Protein: 24–32g
- **Modifications possible:**
  - "less sauce" — sometimes
  - "smaller rice" — always
- **Heuristics:** sweet glaze hidden sugar; fattier beef cut
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Donburi (general — chicken, tuna, oyako)
- **Components:** rice + topping (chicken-egg simmered, raw tuna, salmon roe, etc.) + sweet soy glaze on most
- **Macros (chicken oyakodon ~400g):**
  - Calories: 520–680 kcal
  - Fat: 12–20g (sat 4–6g)
  - Carbs: 75–90g (sugar 8–14g)
  - Protein: 26–34g
- **Heuristics:** sweet glaze sugar load
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM (variant-dependent)

### Onsen / 63° egg (slow-poached)
- **Components:** one egg cooked at 63–68°C
- **Macros:**
  - Calories: 70–90 kcal
  - Fat: 5–6g (sat 1.5g)
  - Protein: 6g
  - Cholesterol: ~186mg (one yolk — relevant for gallbladder / dyslipidemia)
- **Heuristics:** yolk cholesterol watch for relevant profiles
- **Confidence:** HIGH

## Common red flags in this cuisine

- **Tonkotsu and other emulsified-broth ramens** — pork-bone fat is suspended in the broth, can't be skimmed
- **Mazesoba / abura soba oil pool** — invisible 2–3 tbsp oil at the bottom; the lever is "don't scrape"
- **Tempura / tonkatsu / karaage / agedashi / korokke** — deep-fried category
- **Curry rice (kare-raisu)** — roux-based, hidden flour + butter + sugar
- **Spicy mayo on poke / spicy rolls** — mayo-based "spicy" sauces are heavy
- **Sweet glazes on yakitori (tare), donburi, eel (unagi)** — added sugar plus sodium
- **Cured/preserved fish** (himono) — high sodium
- **Wagyu / fatty tuna in large portions** — quality fat but caloric

---

## Common safe choices

- **Sashimi or nigiri of lean fish** — best baseline; salmon adds omega-3
- **Miso soup + chawanmushi** — light, warming, low calorie
- **Clear-broth ramen (shio, shoyu)** — without drinking all the broth
- **Saba / gindara saikyo-yaki** — grilled fatty fish (omega-3), miso marinade is sweet but manageable
- **Yakitori on shio (salt), lean cuts** — breast, heart, gizzard skewers
- **Seaweed salad, edamame** — sides; low calorie, fiber/protein
